I developed the **Heteroscedastic Personalized Regression (Het-PR)** framework, which reformulates personalized modeling as a linear mixed-effects model with sample-specific variance terms to estimate individualized SNP effect profiles. In extensive simulations, Het-PR reduced estimation MSE by roughly 30–60% relative to population-level models, achieved 82–94% AUROC in identifying subject-specific genetic effects, and recovered latent patient subgroups with about 90–100% accuracy via hierarchical clustering.

Applied to the ADNI GWAS cohort, Het-PR stratified Alzheimer's patients into two genetically distinct clusters with consistently divergent cognitive performance across five domains, linking fine-scale genetic signatures to the severity of cognitive impairment. Pathway enrichment of cluster-specific SNP sets revealed distinct functional architectures, and variants previously associated with epilepsy were found to powerfully separate the two clusters — suggesting a shared cross-disease genetic axis between Alzheimer's and epilepsy.
