This project develops a covariate-similarity-driven multi-graph approach that models interference through covariate-based similarity graphs, extending applicability to both known- and unknown-network settings. I formulate multi-dimensional exposure vectors to capture heterogeneous spillovers, negative peer effects, and nonlinear interference via linear and neural-network forms (using tanh and PReLU activations), and introduce average treatment effects defined by treatment intensity to evaluate effects under varying coverage levels — generalizing beyond traditional all-or-none contrasts.

Large-scale simulations on Erdős–Rényi and covariate-driven graphs (n = 4000) demonstrate near-zero bias and improved performance relative to SNIPE, least-squares, and difference-in-means estimators.
